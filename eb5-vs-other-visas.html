EB-5 vs. H1B, O-1, L-1, and EB-2/EB-3

Every U.S. immigration path trades off differently on sponsorship, timeline, and certainty. Here's how EB-5 compares to the routes most often considered alongside it.

EB-5 vs. H1B

FactorH1BEB-5
Status typeTemporary, employer-tiedDirect path to permanent residency
Employer sponsorshipRequiredNot required
Subject to annual lotteryYesNo
Renewable/time-limitedYes (typically 6-year max)No — leads to permanent Green Card

EB-5 vs. EB-2 / EB-3 (employment-based green cards)

FactorEB-2 / EB-3EB-5
BasisJob offer + labor certification (PERM)Investment + job creation
Employer sponsorshipRequiredNot required
Country-of-birth backlogSevere for India/China in most casesShorter, especially reserved categories
Dependent on continued employmentYes, until green card issuedNo

EB-5 vs. O-1 (extraordinary ability)

FactorO-1EB-5
Eligibility basisExtraordinary ability/achievement in a fieldInvestment capital + job creation
Status typeTemporary, renewableDirect path to permanent residency
Employer/agent sponsorshipRequiredNot required

EB-5 vs. L-1 (intracompany transferee)

FactorL-1EB-5
Eligibility basisEmployment with a qualifying multinational companyInvestment capital + job creation
Status typeTemporary (can lead to EB-1C green card in some cases)Direct path to permanent residency
Dependent on employer relationshipYesNo

What stays constant across every comparison

Across all four comparisons, EB-5's defining trade-off is the same: you commit real capital that must remain at risk, in exchange for a path to a Green Card that does not depend on an employer, a lottery, or ongoing job performance. For investors who can document lawful source of funds for the required investment, that trade-off is often worth the independence — particularly for applicants born in countries with long employment-based backlogs.
