EB-5 for Dubai and UAE-Based Investors

Dubai and the wider UAE are home to a large, mobile, and often highly liquid investor and expatriate community. For many, EB-5 is attractive precisely because it offers something the UAE's own residency system doesn't: a direct path to permanent, unconditional U.S. residency for an entire family.

🇦🇪 UAE nationals & expat residents both eligible 🏗️ Asset-backed healthcare real estate 🕐 GST-friendly consultation scheduling

Why Gulf-based investors choose EB-5

The UAE's Golden Visa program offers long-term residency, but it does not confer U.S. immigration status, and it doesn't solve the underlying goal for many families: access to U.S. education, healthcare, and career mobility for children, or a long-term base outside the region. EB-5 is one of the only investment-based routes to a full, unconditional U.S. Green Card — not a temporary visa — for the investor, their spouse, and unmarried children under 21.

Because Dubai's population is majority expatriate, we work with two distinct groups: UAE and other GCC nationals investing directly, and the large expat community (particularly from India, Pakistan, the Philippines, Egypt, and the UK) using UAE-based assets and banking relationships to fund their investment. Eligibility and any visa backlog considerations are generally based on the investor's country of birth, not their country of residence — Indian-born expats living in Dubai, for example, should also review our India-specific guidance.

Structuring the investment: asset-backed and transparent

Healthcare real estate resonates with many Gulf investors because it is a tangible, operating asset producing income from an essential service — not a speculative security. Every xHealth Capital offering includes:

  • The underlying real property and facility being financed
  • A licensed, experienced healthcare operator with a specific business plan
  • Independent job-creation methodology prepared by a qualified economist
  • Full financial projections and construction/operating milestones

A note on Sharia-compliance

Some investors ask whether an EB-5 offering can be structured to align with Islamic finance principles. This depends on the specific offering's debt/equity structure and return mechanism, and should be reviewed directly with our team and your own Sharia advisor before committing capital — we're happy to walk through the structure of any project you're considering.

Moving funds from the UAE compliantly

The UAE has no capital controls comparable to some other jurisdictions, which simplifies outward transfers, but U.S. banks apply enhanced due diligence ("EDD") to large incoming international wires — especially from the Gulf region. To avoid delays:

  • Wire funds from an account held in the investor's own name, matching the name on the EB-5 subscription documents
  • Prepare source-of-funds documentation in advance: business ownership records, audited financials, property sale agreements, salary/employment records, or brokerage statements
  • Work with a bank and immigration attorney experienced in cross-border EB-5 transfers from the UAE specifically

Full documentation standards are covered in our source-of-funds guide.
