Overview

What is the EB-5 Visa?

The EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program is a U.S. employment-based visa category that provides a path to permanent residency for investors and their immediate family in exchange for making a qualifying investment in a new commercial enterprise that creates jobs in the United States. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

For investments filed under current rules, most investors must place a minimum of $1,050,000 into a qualifying project, or $800,000 if the project is located in a Targeted Employment Area (TEA) such as a rural or high-unemployment area, or in certain infrastructure projects. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Core requirements

Key elements of a qualifying EB-5 investment

  • Investment into a new commercial enterprise (NCE) formed for ongoing business activity.
  • Investment meets the minimum capital requirement based on location and filing date.
  • Investment is “at risk” with a chance for both gain and loss.
  • Creation (or preservation) of at least 10 full-time U.S. jobs for qualifying workers. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Compliance with detailed source-of-funds documentation and program integrity rules.

Investors first receive a conditional green card. After roughly two years, they must prove that the capital remained invested and that the required jobs were created before conditions can be removed and full permanent residency is granted. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Why healthcare

Why healthcare fits the EB-5 program

Healthcare projects are often a strong fit for EB-5 capital because they combine real-world community impact with meaningful job creation:

  • Clinics, urgent care centers, and ambulatory surgery centers generate direct clinical and administrative roles.
  • Ancillary services and integrated platforms, such as in-house labs, imaging, and digital health infrastructure, add further headcount.
  • Many healthcare facilities can be located in TEAs, helping investors qualify at the lower investment threshold while serving underserved communities. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
