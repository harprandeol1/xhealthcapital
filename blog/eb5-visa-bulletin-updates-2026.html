Visa Bulletin

How to Read the EB-5 Visa Bulletin in 2026

Category: Visa BulletinBy xHealth Capital Research Team

Every month, the U.S. Department of State publishes the Visa Bulletin — the document that tells applicants in backlogged categories whether their priority date is eligible to move forward. For EB-5 investors, understanding this document is essential to setting realistic expectations for your Green Card timeline.

Two charts, two purposes

The bulletin publishes two separate charts each month for employment-based categories:

  • Final Action Dates: the date that determines when a visa can actually be issued, or when Adjustment of Status can be approved.
  • Dates for Filing: an earlier, often more generous date that determines when applicants may submit an Adjustment of Status application (when USCIS permits use of this chart) or begin gathering documents for consular processing.

USCIS announces monthly which chart applicants adjusting status inside the U.S. may use — always confirm the current chart before assuming your eligibility.

What "Current" (C) means

When a category/country combination shows "C," it means there is no backlog — visas are immediately available to all qualified applicants in that category, regardless of priority date. Most countries are current in the EB-5 categories most of the time; the exceptions are mainland China, and at various points India and Vietnam.

Reserved categories change the math

Since the EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act, a portion of the annual EB-5 visa allocation is reserved specifically for:

  • Rural area projects
  • High-unemployment area projects
  • Infrastructure projects

These reserved visas are tracked separately in the bulletin from the general, unreserved EB-5 category. Because the reserved pools are newer and less subscribed, they have generally remained current or shown much shorter backlogs than the general category — a major reason we concentrate our healthcare projects in rural TEAs.

Practical takeaway for investors

If you're from a country with a history of EB-5 backlogs, prioritizing a rural TEA project isn't just about the lower $800,000 investment threshold — it can also mean a materially shorter path to your visa. Review our program requirements guide and processing time guide for the fuller picture, and always verify current bulletin data before making investment decisions based on timeline.
