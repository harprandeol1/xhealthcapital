How It Works

Your Path From Investment to Green Card

  1. 1

    Free Consultation & Eligibility Review

    We assess your immigration goals, timeline, and source-of-funds profile to confirm EB-5 fit.

  2. 2

    Select a Healthcare Project

    Review offering documents for our current healthcare sectors and choose the project that matches your risk and timeline preferences.

  3. 3

    Source of Funds & Investment

    Your immigration attorney prepares source-of-funds documentation while you complete the subscription and capital transfer.

  4. 4

    File Form I-526E

    Your attorney files the immigrant petition, which includes the healthcare project's business plan and job-creation methodology.

  5. 5

    Conditional Green Card

    Upon approval, you and your qualifying family members receive conditional permanent residency.

  6. 6

    File Form I-829 & Remove Conditions

    After job-creation requirements are documented, you file to remove conditions and receive a 10-year Green Card.

Frequently Asked Questions

Healthcare EB-5 FAQs

Healthcare real estate combines non-discretionary community demand with strong staffing-driven job creation — two factors that directly support both the EB-5 job-creation requirement and long-term facility viability. Focusing exclusively on this sector lets our team build deep underwriting expertise in medical licensing, reimbursement models, and healthcare operators.

Under current law, the minimum investment is $800,000 for projects in a qualifying Targeted Employment Area (TEA) — typically rural or high-unemployment areas — and $1,050,000 for non-TEA projects. Most of our healthcare projects are located in rural TEAs to take advantage of the lower threshold and priority processing. See our full 2026 requirements guide for details.

Each EB-5 investor must be credited with at least 10 full-time qualifying jobs. Healthcare facilities generally support strong direct job counts (clinicians, nurses, technicians, administrative staff) plus indirect and induced jobs in regional-center-sponsored projects, giving many of our offerings a healthy job-creation buffer above the minimum requirement.

Yes. EB-5 is available to any foreign national who can document lawful source of funds, regardless of current visa status. F1 students and H1B holders frequently use EB-5 to secure permanent residency independent of employer sponsorship or visa lottery outcomes. See our student guide or H1B guide.

Yes. A successful EB-5 petition can lead to a Green Card for the principal investor, their spouse, and unmarried children under 21.

Yes — EB-5 law requires that capital remain genuinely "at risk" with a real possibility of gain or loss. We provide full offering documents, financial projections, and operator track records so investors can evaluate that risk before committing capital.
